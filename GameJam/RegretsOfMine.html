Overview

Regrets of Mine is a 3D narrative-driven first-person experience about memory, loss, and the things we never truly leave behind. You return to a Victorian house you once called home. By finding different masks, you gain access to shift how the world reveals itself - paintings change, spaces distort, and hidden memories resurface. Each mask exposes a different layer of the past, asking you to confront relationships shaped by duty, absence, and silence. There is no combat. No rush. Progress comes from observation, reflection, and understanding what the house is trying to show you. This game is about facing regret - not to erase it, but to finally let it rest.

Responsibilities

  • Implemented a stencil-buffer-based post-processing effect enabling selective visibility of objects when viewed through mirrors.
  • Utilized Scene Capture 2D to render mirrored perspectives.
  • Designed and implemented gameplay mechanics centered around mirror-based puzzles and player interaction.

Technical Implementation

  • Custom stencil masking for object visibility control.
  • Scene Capture 2D integration for real-time mirror rendering.
  • Post-process material setup for layered rendering control.

What I Learned

Gained practical experience working with custom stencil buffers and render targets. Improved understanding of multi-pass rendering and scene capture workflows required for mirror-based effects.
